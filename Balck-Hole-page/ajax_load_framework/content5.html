The future and Black Holes?

If you have watched any science fiction movie centered around space, you may have seen the characters using a Black Hole to travel through space or even through time. This may not be plausible because as we recently discussed, our theories tell us that if anything were to enter a Black Hole, the object's atoms would be stretched followed by gaining extreme heat before being subsequently destroyed. This is just a theory though. Black Holes may help us gain the capabilities necessary to achieve feats we never thought possible.

Just a few years ago, physicist Jeff Steinhauer from Technion University in Haifa, Israel, was able to create a Black Hole from manipulating sound. This year, NASA created and publicly released a computer-generated visual of how gravity warps our view of Black Holes, which can be found here. This is a testament to our growth technologically as a species. We went from having limited knowledge derived from outdated observation tools to capturing real images of objects in space. If you are interested in learning more about Black Holes, visit NASA's page on Black Holes.
